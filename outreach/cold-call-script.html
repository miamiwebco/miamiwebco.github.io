Cold Call Script
Miami Restaurant Websites

Nicholas
Miami, FL
One-time build · $500
Step 1 Opening
You
"Hey, is this [Restaurant Name]?"
Them
Yes
You
"Hi, my name's Nicholas — I'm local, over here in Miami. Quick question — do you guys have a website I could check out?"
Them
No / We don't have one
You
"Yeah, that's what I figured. So I do web design for local restaurants here in Miami, and I saw you guys on Google — you've got people coming in, clearly doing something right — but there's no website, so I went ahead and put together a quick demo for you. Free, no strings. I just figured it's easier to show you something real than talk about it."
You
"Do you have 30 seconds? I can text you the link right now while we're on the phone."
Step 2 Uncover the Problem
You
"Can I ask — right now, how do most new customers find you? Like people who've never been before."
Them
Word of mouth / walk-ins / social media
You
"Yeah, that's how most spots operate. How long have you been going with just that?"
You
"And honestly — does that feel like it's growing, or has it kind of plateaued?"
Step 3 The Value Moment
You
"Here's the thing — when someone in your neighborhood pulls out their phone and searches 'restaurants near me' or 'best food in Allapattah,' they're not finding you. They're finding whoever has a website. Google skips right past businesses with no site."
You
"What do you think your average table spends when they come in?"
Them
Say they answer $40
You
"Okay so if just 10 new tables a month found you through Google — that's $400 extra a month, $4,800 a year, just from showing up in search. And that's being conservative. Right now that's just not happening because there's nothing for Google to find."
Step 4 The Close
You
"So what I put together for you has your menu, hours, address, contact form — everything. Looks professional. I can send the link right now and you can pull it up while we're talking."
Do it
Send the demo link NOW. Don't wait.
You
"If you like it, I can make it live with your real info for $500, one time. No subscriptions, nothing monthly. It's yours."
If they ask why you built it for free: "Honestly I'd rather just show you than tell you. Easier for both of us."
Step 5 Objection Responses
"I'm not interested."
"Totally fair. Can I just text you the demo link anyway? You don't have to do anything with it — but if you ever change your mind, at least you've seen what it could look like. Takes two seconds."
→ Most people say yes. Now you have a warm lead to follow up with.
"I already have someone handling that."
"Oh nice — do you have a site up already then? I might have just missed it."
→ If yes: wish them well, hang up gracefully.
→ If no / "we're working on it": "Got it, I won't waste your time. If it ever doesn't work out with them, I'm local and quick — can I text you my number just in case?"
"I don't have time for this."
"I hear you — I'll be quick. I'm not asking you to do anything right now. I just want to text you the link. You can look at it tonight when it's slow, or whenever. Thirty seconds to receive a text — that's all I'm asking."
"Just send me an email."
"I can do that — what's your email?"
→ Get it, send immediately with the demo link in the body.
→ Follow up in 48 hours: "Hey, just checking you got that — did you get a chance to look at the demo?"
"$500 is too much."
"I get it. Think about it this way — you make that back in two or three extra tables that found you online. After that, every customer that walks in from Google is pure profit, every month, for as long as the site is up."
→ If still hesitant: "If $500 is tight right now, I can do two payments of $250. Whatever works."
"I need to think about it."
"One hundred percent, no pressure. Can I at least send you the demo link so you have something to actually look at while you're thinking it over? A lot of people find it easier to decide once they can see the real thing."
→ Send it, then: "I'll check back in a couple days — what's the best time to reach you, mornings or afternoons?" Lock in a specific callback time. "I'll think about it" without a follow-up time = dead lead.
"We use social media, that's enough."
"Social media is great for people who already follow you. A website is how strangers find you — people who've never heard of you, searching on Google right now. They're two different things. You really want both."

Before You Dial Have the demo URL copied and ready to paste. The moment they say "okay show me" is the hottest second of the call — don't fumble it.
Tone Slow down. Confident, not pushy. You're a local guy doing them a favor, not a telemarketer hitting a quota. Smile — it comes through on the phone.
Always Get a Next Step Never hang up without a yes, a link sent, an email, or a specific callback time. "I'll think about it" with nothing locked in is a no.
