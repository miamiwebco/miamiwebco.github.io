📞 Cold Call Script

Connection Problem Consequence Solution Objections
Phase 1 — Connection Don't sound like a salesperson
You
"Hello, hi — I was actually looking at your restaurant on Google. Do you guys have a website I could check out real quick?"
Them
No / We don't have one
You
"Oh okay, I wasn't sure — sometimes they're hard to find. Hey, is this the owner or manager by any chance?"
Them
Yes, I'm the owner / manager
You
"Perfect. I'll be honest with you — the reason I'm calling is I work with local Miami restaurants and I noticed you guys didn't have one. I'm not trying to sell you anything right now, I'm just curious about something if you have like 2 minutes."
Phase 2 — Problem Awareness Let them feel it
You
"How are people usually discovering your restaurant right now — like someone who's never been before?"
Them
Word of mouth, walk-ins, Instagram…
You
"Yeah that makes sense. And how long have you guys been relying mainly on that?"
Them
Since we opened / a few years…
You
"Got it. And when someone hears about you and wants to check you out before coming — like look at the menu, see the vibe, check your hours — where do they go right now?"
Let the silence sit. Don't fill it. They're doing the work for you.
Phase 3 — Consequence Make them do the math
You
"I ask because — and tell me if I'm wrong — but right now if someone searches 'restaurants near me' in your area on their phone, your competitors with websites are showing up first. You're basically invisible to anyone who doesn't already know you exist. Does that sound about right?"
Them
Yeah… that's probably true
You
"How many new customers a week would you say actually find you for the first time right now?"
Them
They give a number
You
"So what do you think you're missing out on — just from the people searching on Google who never find you?"
Let them say the number. Don't say it for them. Whatever they say is more powerful than anything you could quote.
Phase 4 — Solution Awareness The demo, not the pitch
You
"Here's why I actually called — I went ahead and built you a free demo, just to show you what it could look like. No commitment, nothing to pay. I just figured it's easier to show you than explain it. Would it be alright if I sent you the link right now while we're talking?"
Them
Sure / yeah go ahead
You
"I'm sending it now — just let me know when you see it."
Wait. Don't say anything else. Let them open the link.
You
"What do you think?"
Stop talking. Let them react. The next person who speaks loses.
If They Like It — Transition to Close
You
"Glad you like it. So the way this works — it's a one-time fee to build it out fully with your real menu, photos, everything. It's $500 for the full restaurant site. After that it's yours. If you ever want updates there's an optional monthly thing but most people just do the one-time. Does that sound reasonable?"
Then stop. Let them answer. Don't pre-justify the price.
Objections Tap to expand
Objection
"I'm not interested."
▼
"Totally fair. Can I just ask — is it more that the timing isn't right, or that you don't really see the value in having a website for the restaurant?"
→ Either answer tells you exactly what to address next. Don't defend. Just ask.
Objection
"I already have someone handling that."
▼
"Oh nice — where's the site at? I'd love to check it out."
→ If they have one: wish them well, hang up clean.
→ If they don't / "we're working on it": "Got it — how long has that been in progress?"
Objection
"I don't have time right now."
▼
"No worries at all. When's a better time to call — would tomorrow morning work, or is afternoon better for you?"
→ Always offer two specific options. "I'll call you back sometime" is not a follow-up time.
Objection
"Just send me an email."
▼
"Of course — what's your email?"
→ Get it. Send the demo link immediately with a one-line message.
→ Follow up in 48 hours: "Hey, just wanted to make sure you got that — did you get a chance to look at the demo?"
Objection
"$500 is too much."
▼
"What would make it feel worth it to you?"
→ Let them set the frame. If they push back on value, not just price:

"Think about it this way — if the site brings in two extra tables a month, you've made it back. After that it's pure profit every month for as long as it's up. Does that math make sense?"
Objection
"I need to think about it."
▼
"Of course — what part is it that you're still unsure about?"
→ "Think about it" usually means one specific hesitation they haven't named. Get them to say it.

→ Then: "Can I send you the demo link so you have something to actually look at while you're thinking? Easier to decide when you can see the real thing."

→ Lock in a callback time before hanging up.
Objection
"We get enough customers already."
▼
"That's great — so would you say the business is exactly where you want it, or is there still room to grow?"
→ Almost no owner will say they're maxed out. Their own answer opens the door.
Objection
"We use Instagram / social media."
▼
"Yeah social is great. Curious — when someone searches 'restaurants near me' on Google, does your Instagram show up there, or just your profile page?"
→ They'll realize the answer is no. Let them arrive at that themselves.
Golden Rule Never answer an objection directly. Respond with a question.
